History

Tokyo, origionally called Edo, has been the capital of Japan since 1603. In 1923, nearly 45% of the city was detroyed in the Great Kanto earthquake. Then during WWII, the 1945 Bombing of Tokyo resulted in the death of almost half the city's population. Since WWII, Tokyo has rebuilt, hosted the Olympics, and become the birthplace of karaoke, nintendo, sega and some of your favorite characters such as: mario, hello kitty, pikachu, and anpanman.

Unique Historical Facts:

  • Tokyo's imperial palace is home to the oldest remaining monarchy in the world.
  • It is the site of the deadliest air raid to this date.
  • Tokyo hosted the Olympics once in 1964, and was meant to host them again in 2020.

More information on Tokyo's history: history.com

Culture

From the ancient arts of kabuki theatre, origami, and sumo wrestling to the high tech bullet train system, Tokyo's culture is a blend of the modern and traditional. Some common celebrations in tokyo are New Years (Shogatsu), the cherry blossom festival (Hanami), and Obon, an celebration to guide dead loved ones home for three days out of the year.

Unique Cultural Traits:

  • It is the largest city in the world.
  • It is the safest city in the world.
  • It is the most technologically developed city in the world.

More information on Tokyo's culture: globalizationpartners.com
